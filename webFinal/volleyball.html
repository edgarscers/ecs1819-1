Volleyball

Volleyball was created in 1895 by William G. Morgan. The game was created to be a combination of basketball, baseball, tennis, and handball. After years of playing, USVBA was formed in 1928 when rules need to be clear for tournament play.

THE BASICS

Passing is usually the first part of a team's play when recieving the ball. Passing is performed when recieving a serve or an attack by the opponent. It is important to keep a strong platform, straight arms and angle towards the target. On serve recieve, the two Outside Hitters and the Libero are in the backrow passing.
Each team attempts to pass the ball to the setter and the setter have the second touch of the play. There are many different types of sets that are performed by the setter. The most basic sets are called 1,9,A,B,Pipe. The setter can set either the Outside Hitter, Middle, Right Side, or the other Outside (playing in the backrow). A successful set that results in a kill from the hitter is called an assist.
The last touch of a team's play is usually a hit. This comes after a set or pass by the setter or another teamate. A team's hit is challenged by the opponents' blockers. The hitter can hit cross court, line (the same sideline), or tool the block (hit off of the block). A successful hit that results in a point is called a kill.

THE COURT

The dimensions for a volleyball court are 60 feet by 30 feet. The center line is in the middle of the court dividing each teams' playing side. 10 feet away from the net on each side is the attack line. Players in the backrow cannot jump inside of the 10 foot line. The service line is 10 feet behind the endline. Antennas align with the court sdielines. The net's height for women is 7 feet 4 inches and for men is 7 feet 11 inches. There are 6 players on each teams' side. 3 in the front row and 3 in the backrow.
